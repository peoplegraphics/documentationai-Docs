PanelWave is building a community layer around published works: comments, reactions, likes, watchlists, and social sharing. This page describes what that toolkit contains — and, honestly, how much of it you can use right now.

<Callout kind="alert">
  **Current status:** the community building blocks below are implemented in the platform but are **not yet visible anywhere in the CMS or reader experience**. There is no screen where creators or readers interact with comments or likes today, and no moderation dashboard exists yet. Treat this page as a preview of what is coming rather than a guide to something you can click.
</Callout>

## What the toolkit includes

### Comments

A threaded comment section designed to attach to a work, a chapter, or an individual panel:

- Readers write comments (up to 5,000 characters) and post replies one level deep.
- Sorting by **Newest**, **Popular**, or **Oldest**.
- Six emoji reactions on any comment: 👍 like, ❤️ love, 😂 laugh, 😮 surprised, 😢 sad, 😠 angry.
- Authors can **Edit** and **Delete** their own comments (edited comments are labeled).
- Comments load in pages of 20 with a **Load More Comments** button.
- New comments can be held back for review — the author then sees *"Your comment is pending moderation."* A creator-facing moderation view (approving, rejecting, or pinning comments) is planned but does not exist yet; pinned comments can already be displayed with a **Pinned** badge.

### Likes

A heart button for works and individual panels, with a running count (abbreviated as 1.2K / 3.4M for large numbers). Liking requires a signed-in reader.

### Watchlist

A star button that lets readers follow a work: **Add to Watchlist** / **Remove from Watchlist**, with an option to be notified when the work is updated, plus a follower count. This is the "following" mechanism for serialized works.

### Sharing

A share menu for a work, chapter, or panel offering **Twitter**, **Facebook**, **Reddit**, **Email**, and **Copy Link**, along with a share counter ("Shared 12 times").
