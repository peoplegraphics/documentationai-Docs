**Extras** are bonus content that readers can browse alongside your story: covers, concept art, author interviews, behind-the-scenes material. Open the **Extras Manager** from the Backstage sidebar of a work.

## Content buckets

Extras are organized into eight tabs (buckets), each with its own count:

| Tab | Purpose |
|---|---|
| **Cover** | Main cover image |
| **Alt Covers** | Alternative cover art |
| **Character Sheets** | Character reference sheets (each linked to a [character](/cms/characters)) |
| **Author Info** | About the author |
| **Interviews** | Author interviews |
| **Bonus Art** | Bonus artwork and panels |
| **Fan Art** | Community fan art |
| **Behind the Scenes** | Process, sketches, soundtrack |

A stats bar tracks totals across all buckets: **Total**, **Gated**, **Shareable**, **Total Views**, and **Downloads**.

## Adding and editing an extra

Click **Add Extra** (the new extra lands in the currently selected tab). The dialog contains:

- **Title** (required) and an optional **Description** — these are the default-language fallbacks.
- **Translations** — pick a locale and enter a localized title and description; empty fields fall back to the defaults. See [Localization](/cms/localization).
- **Content Type** — Image, Video, Audio, PDF, or Text. For Text you write the content directly. For media types the content can come from two sources:
  - **Browse library…** opens the [asset picker](/cms/assets#picking-assets-anywhere) — choose an existing asset from the work's library or upload a new file right in the dialog. The linked asset shows as a compact chip (thumbnail, filename, and an **×** to unlink); dimensions, duration, and file size are filled in automatically.
  - Or enter an **external URL or path** (validated to start with `http(s)://`, `/`, or `./`) — for content hosted elsewhere.
- **Status** — **Published** or **Draft (hidden from readers)**. Drafts stay visible in the manager but are **excluded from the exported manifest** and from the reader preview.
- **Character** — required when the extra lives in the **Character Sheets** bucket. Sheets without a linked character are dropped from the export.
- **Poster** (video) or **Thumbnail** (everything else), optional — likewise a library pick or an external URL.
- **Width/Height** for images, **Duration** for audio/video.
- Three toggles:
  - **Requires Entitlement (Gated)** — the extra is locked behind a purchase; set the **Required Tier** (e.g. `premium`). See [Monetization](/cms/monetization).
  - **Shareable** — readers may share a link to it.
  - **Downloadable** — readers may download the file.

Click **Create Extra** / **Update Extra** to save.

<Callout kind="tip">
The gated, shareable, and downloadable toggles are also available directly on each card — no need to open the editor for quick changes.
</Callout>

<Callout kind="info">
Prefer linking a **library asset** over pasting its URL: the extra stays connected to the asset, so **Replace File** in the [Assets library](/cms/assets) updates it automatically, and readers always receive a fresh, working link. External URLs remain fully supported for content hosted outside PanelWave.
</Callout>

## Managing the grid

Each extra card shows its thumbnail (with **Gated** and **Draft** badges where relevant), description, content type, file size, dimensions or duration, and view/download counts. From the card you can:

- **Preview** — click the thumbnail (images, video, and audio play inline).
- **Reorder** — drag the grip handle, or use the move up/down buttons; the order is what readers see.
- **Edit** and **Delete**.

## Previewing as a reader

**Preview as reader** opens a full-screen view of the extras section exactly as it will appear to readers:

- Grouped by bucket, **drafts excluded**.
- A **Locale** switcher shows localized titles/descriptions.
- Gated extras render locked ("Unlock to view"), including the required tier.

## Draft vs. published

Use **Draft** status for anything you're still working on — fan-art submissions under review, an interview awaiting approval. The workflow is: create as draft → review in **Preview as reader** → flip to **Published** → the extra is included the next time you [publish](/cms/publishing) or [export](/cms/export).

Extras end up in the `extras` section of the manifest — see the [Extras schema reference](/schema/extras).
