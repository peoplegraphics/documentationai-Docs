---
title: Hotspots
description: Draw rectangle, circle, and polygon hotspots, attach actions and mutations, and gate them with variable conditions in the PanelWave editor.
---

Hotspots are the clickable areas that make your graphic novel interactive: a door the reader can open, a clue they can inspect, a choice that branches the story. You draw a hotspot on a panel, then attach **actions** (what happens on click), optional **mutations** (dynamic visual changes), and an optional **visibility condition**.

## Drawing a hotspot

Hotspots come in three shapes, each with its own drawing tool:

| Shape | Tool | Shortcut | What you get |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Rectangle** | Rectangle | `R` | A rectangle centered on your click |
| **Circle** | Circle | `C` | A circle centered on your click |
| **Polygon** | Pen | `P` | A starter polygon whose vertices you reshape |

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open a panel">
    Hotspots are drawn in panel view — double-click a panel on the [canvas](/cms/editor/canvas) to enter it.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Pick a shape tool">
    Select **Rectangle**, **Circle**, or **Pen** from the tool palette — or right-click the canvas and choose **Add Hotspot** → **Rectangle** / **Circle** / **Polygon**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Click the canvas">
    The hotspot appears at the click point, drawn as a dashed, lightly tinted overlay with its label above it. It is selected and ready to edit.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout kind="info">
  The dashed overlay is an editing aid — readers never see it. In the published work the area is simply clickable.
</Callout>

## Editing the shape

Select a hotspot (click it with the shape tool, or **Tab** / **Shift+Tab** to cycle through a panel's hotspots) and drag its control points:

- **Rectangle** — 8 handles: corners resize from that corner, edge handles move that side.
- **Circle** — a center handle moves the whole circle; a radius handle scales it.
- **Polygon** — every vertex is a draggable handle. In the Inspector's **Shape (polygon)** section you can also type exact coordinates for each point, remove a point (a polygon keeps at least 3), or click **+ Add Point**.

The Inspector's **Shape** section always mirrors the canvas: for rectangles it shows **X**, **Y**, **Width**, **Height**; for circles **Center X**, **Center Y**, **Radius** — the values sync with your canvas edits.

## Naming and accessibility

In **Basic Properties**:

- **Label** — the hotspot's name, edited per language (the field shows the active locale, e.g. **Label (en-US)**; switch locales in the editor header — see [Localization](/cms/localization)).
- **ARIA Label** — a screen-reader description, so interactive areas stay accessible.
- **Visible** and **Locked** — the usual editing toggles.

## Actions — what happens on click

The **Actions** section lists the hotspot's actions. Click **+ Add Action** to open the Action Builder, pick an **Action Type**, fill in its settings, and save. A hotspot can carry several actions; each shows a summary with **Edit** and **Remove** buttons.

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Navigate" icon="arrow-right">
    **Navigate (Go To Panel/Page)** jumps the reader elsewhere in the story. Choose a **Target Type** (Panel or Page), enter the **Panel ID** or **Page Number**, and pick a **Transition Effect**: None, Fade, Slide, Zoom, or Crossfade. This is the building block for choices and branching — see [Graph](/cms/editor/graph) for how branches fit into your story flow.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Set Variables" icon="variable">
    **Set Variables** changes story state. Add one or more rows, each with a variable name, an operation, and a value:
    - **Set** — assign the value
    - **Increment** — add to a number
    - **Toggle** — flip a true/false value

    This is how a hotspot remembers things ("the reader took the key", "score + 10"). Manage the variables themselves in the [Variables designer](/cms/editor/variables).
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Open Extras" icon="gift">
    **Open Extras** shows a bonus-content item. Enter the **Extras ID** of the content you created in the [Extras manager](/cms/extras).
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Open Modal" icon="panel-top">
    **Open Modal** pops up a dialog over the story. Set a **Modal ID** and the **Modal Content** text.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Trigger Plugin Event" icon="plug">
    **Trigger Plugin Event** sends an event to a plugin. Pick the **Plugin ID** (Image Zoom, 360° Panorama, or Gallery), an **Event Name**, and optional **Event Data** as JSON.
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

## Mutations — dynamic visual changes

Mutations change how the hotspot itself looks or behaves in response to the reader. Click **+ Add Mutation** in the **Mutations** section, choose a **Trigger Event**, and add one or more property mutations:

- **Triggers:** **On Hover**, **On Click**, or **When Variable Changes** (the last one takes a condition expression, e.g. `hasKey === true`).
- **Properties you can mutate:** Visible, Opacity, Scale, Rotation, Position, Color — each with a target value, a **Duration (ms)**, and an **Easing** curve (Linear, Ease, Ease In, Ease Out, Ease In Out).

A typical use: a subtle **On Hover** scale-up so readers can discover what's clickable.

## Transition

The **Transition** section styles the moment the hotspot fires: an **Effect** (None, Fade, Slide, Zoom, Crossfade), a **Duration (ms)**, and an **Easing** function.

## Conditions — showing hotspots only sometimes

The **Visibility Condition** section makes a hotspot appear only when your story state allows it. You can type an expression into **Visible If** directly (for example `variable.hasKey === true`), but the friendlier way is the **Build Condition** button, which opens the Condition Builder:

1. Click **+ Add Condition** and fill in the **Variable** name, its **Type** (String, Number, Boolean), an **Operator** (equals, not equals; greater/less than for numbers; contains, starts with, ends with for strings), and a **Value**.
2. With two or more conditions, choose how to combine them: **AND (all must be true)** or **OR (any can be true)**.
3. The **Expression Preview** shows the resulting expression as you build. Click **Apply Condition** to save it to the hotspot.

You don't need to know any syntax — the builder writes the expression for you. Under the hood, conditions evaluate against the variables of your work; read [Variables in the editor](/cms/editor/variables) and [Variables concepts](/concepts/variables) to understand scopes and how state persists for the reader.

<Callout kind="tip">
  Classic pattern: hotspot A runs a **Set Variables** action (`hasKey` → true). Hotspot B, elsewhere in the story, has the visibility condition `variable.hasKey === true` — it only becomes clickable once the reader found the key.
</Callout>

## Finding panels with hotspots

The structure tree's filter bar has a **Show panels with hotspots** toggle, handy for auditing interactivity across a long work. Always test your hotspots in [Preview](/cms/preview) before [publishing](/cms/publishing).
